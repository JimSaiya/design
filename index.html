Weather

The weather in Iceland is very unpredictable. It could be sunny and beautiful in the morning and pouring rain in the afternoon, or vice versa. We are praying to Freyr for good weather but please come prepared.

Packing List

Camping in Iceland Although it may be sunny and nice at day, the cold creeps in at night, especially when lying still inside your tent, dreaming of next day’s adventures. So be smart! And pack the things that will keep you warm!

  • An insulation mattress
  • A good quality sleeping bag (it can drop below zero °C at night)
  • A pillow
  • A pump for the mattress
  • A WARM blanket, makes all the difference!
  • Clothes
  • Bring or buy warm socks – you are going to sleep wearing warm clothes!
  • A woolen sweater over a fleece sweater is a classic combination in Iceland! (Do not underestimate the locals’ advice)
  • Warm stocking cap/wool hat
  • Gloves
  • A good jacket (do not overestimate the Icelandic summer)
  • Bring swimsuits – Iceland has amazing thermal pools and there’s nothing better after a night of camping
  • Second layer pants – since you will mostly be staying outdoors (and we are known for windy days)
  • A bottle for water
  • A thermos for hot water and cups for coffee/tea (indispensable for getting warmth in your body)
  • Hot cocoa/coffee/tea/soup
  • Bring your own knife, fork and spoon that you can wash and reuse during your stay.
  • A kitchen towel will come in handy!
  • A cutting board will also be good to have
  • A flash light is never a bad idea – but the night in late May only has about 4 hours of darkness
